Illustrations of Geometric Fractals

A fractal is a never-ending pattern. Fractals are infinitely complex patterns that are self-similar across different scales. They are created by repeating a simple process over and over in an ongoing feedback loop.
Geometric fractals are created by doing simple iterations on geometric objects; like adding a dent on each side of a tringle, which gives rise to the Famous Koch Snowflake.
I have created a webpage where one can create few of this fractals step by step, and understand the process.

Visualisation of the Mandelbrot Set

A fractal is a never-ending pattern. Fractals are infinitely complex patterns that are self-similar across different scales. They are created by repeating a simple process over and over in an ongoing feedback loop.
Mandelbrot set is a Julia Limit Set.
I have created a webpage where you can visualise this set. You can Zoom in and zoom out and check the various features of this very interesting set.
